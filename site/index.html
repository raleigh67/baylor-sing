Act I

The Sound

What Does Winning Sound Like?

Every Sing act picks songs, arranges a medley, and performs it live. But do winners sound different from the rest? We pulled audio features from Spotify for hundreds of Sing songs and compared first-place acts to the field. Energy is where the gap shows up first.

Valence measures musical positivity — higher means happier-sounding. Winners tend to skew brighter, choosing songs that leave the audience feeling good. But the spread is wide; a well-placed ballad can still take the crown.

Danceability captures rhythm and groove. Winning acts don't just sing — they move. The data shows a consistent danceability edge for top finishers, suggesting choreography-friendly song choices pay off on the Waco Hall stage.

The Sing Jukebox

Some artists just belong on the Sing stage. Over seven decades, certain names keep showing up in medleys — crowd favorites that directors know will land. These are the most-covered artists in Sing history.

How old are the songs that Sing acts choose? Some years lean into classics; others chase the charts. The age of a medley's songs reveals whether an act is playing it safe with nostalgia or betting on something fresh.

Genre DNA of a Medley

Every Sing medley is a cocktail of genres — pop, rock, R&B, country, musical theater. The mix has shifted dramatically over the decades. What once leaned Broadway and barbershop now pulls from Top 40 and hip-hop. This stream shows how Sing's sonic identity has evolved year by year.

Act II

The Color

The Palette of Performance

Sing isn't just heard — it's seen. Costumes, backdrops, and lighting create a visual signature for every act. We extracted dominant colors from performance photos and mapped their brightness and saturation. Do winners dress differently than the rest?

Sound Meets Color

Is there a relationship between how an act sounds and how it looks? We cross-referenced audio features with color palettes to find out. High-energy acts tend toward bolder, more saturated colors — the music and the visual identity reinforce each other.
