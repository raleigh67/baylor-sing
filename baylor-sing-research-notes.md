## Audio Fingerprinting Results (Shazam)

### Method
- Used shazamio library to process YouTube audio downloads
- Split each video into 20-second segments with 5-second overlap
- Ran Shazam on each segment

### Results
- **Videos successfully downloaded**: 7 (1990-2020 era)
- **Videos failed (network error)**: ~28 (YouTube blocked connections after initial downloads)
- **Songs identified by Shazam**: 1 total
  - 2019 Kappa Alpha Theta: "A Walk Through the Graveyard" by Jim Shafer (likely a false positive or obscure sample match)
- **Conclusion**: Shazam is largely ineffective for live collegiate singing performances. The live vocals, rearranged medleys, and audience noise make audio fingerprinting unreliable for this content type. Web research proved far more effective for song identification.

### 2009 First Place Mystery
Despite searching extensively across Baylor news archives, the Baylor Lariat, KOT history, ZTA history, and general web searches, the 2009 first place Sing winner could not be definitively identified. Some search results mention "The Jungle Book" associated with KOT in 2009, but the confirmed KOT placement was 2nd place with "Steppin' to the Badside." The first place winner may have been Pi Beta Phi, Delta Delta Delta, Kappa Kappa Gamma, or another organization. This would require checking the physical Baylor archives or contacting Baylor Student Activities directly.

## Interesting Historical Findings

### Founding
- First Sing: April 25, 1953, organized by Marie Wiley Mathis (Student Union director)
- Originally planned for SUB Bowl outdoors but moved to Waco Hall due to rain
- Only 13 people attended the first show
- Each act sang three songs, at least one about Baylor
- "Peer Club" won the inaugural event

### Growth
- By 1955 (year 2): 17 groups competing
- By 1963 (10th anniversary): 3,000 attendees
- Choreography was added in the 1960s
- By the 1970s, groups were spending thousands of dollars and students were failing classes due to practice time

### Key Rule Changes
- 1987: Co-ed acts allowed; practice limited to 12 hours/week; Thursday shows replaced by Saturday matinees
- Safety rule (~2019): Maximum 200 people on stage
- Act length evolved: 12-13 min (1960s) → 11 min (1980s) → 7 min (current)
- Mary Wiley Mathis directed Sing from 1953 to 1981

### Notable Facts
- 1984 Sigma Chi "Basement Full of Blues" performed at Dallas Mavericks halftime show and NBA All-Star game; Larry Bird requested a photo
- 2022 was the 69th year of Sing
- The KOT Turn dance move originated in the 1970s and continues today
- Pigskin Revue (fall homecoming version) has existed since the 1930s/1940s, predating Sing itself
- KOT, founded 1960, is the winningest organization in Sing history

### Dominant Groups by Era
- **1970s**: Kappa Omega Tau (won almost every year)
- **1980s**: Sigma Chi breakthrough (1984), then KOT resumed dominance
- **1990s**: "Decade of the Dynasties" - Phi Gamma Delta dominated (1993-96), followed by Phi Kappa Chi
- **2000s**: Mixed - DDD, Phi Kappa Chi, KOT, paired acts
- **2010s**: Kappa Omega Tau resurgence (2010, 2013, 2014, 2017, 2019)
- **2020s**: Variety - paired acts (KKG+KSig 2020), KOT (2022, 2024), Chi Omega (2023, 2025), BYX (2026)

## Recommendations for Future Research

1. **Contact Baylor University Archives** (digitalcollectionsinfo@baylor.edu) to request scanned Sing programs from 1953-1969 and other missing years
2. **Access the physical Texas Collection** at Baylor for handwritten annotated sheet music and original programs
3. **Baylor Round-Up yearbooks** from the 1950s-1980s likely contain Sing results and photos
4. **The Baylor Lariat newspaper archives** (physical copies) for pre-digital era issues (1953-2000)
5. **Re-run Shazam** when YouTube access is more stable (the script is ready at shazam-sing-acts.py); consider using a VPN or different network
6. **Check Baylor's digital collections** as they continue adding scanned programs (the collection is actively growing)
7. **Alumni Facebook groups** ("Baylor Sing Alumni", "Baylor Greek Life") may have members who remember results from the 1960s-1980s
